50 Common Interview Questions and Answers

When you've got a job interview coming up, good preparation is essential. By planning and practicing your answers, you can boost your confidence and increase your chances of getting the outcome you want.

Interviewers want to hear about your career history, your attributes and skills, and how your competencies match those specific to the job. They also want to know whether you're a good fit for their team. The more accurately you can predict their questions, the better you can prepare the most persuasive answers.

But how do you know in advance what you'll be asked?

The good news is that interviews tend to follow a common pattern. And most of them use very similar questions. By understanding why interviewers follow some classic lines of inquiry, you can prepare the best possible responses, and have plenty of great examples ready to use.

We've created this resource to help you to get ready for your next interview. It lists the 50 most-asked interview questions, along with advice about preparing great answers for all of them.

50 Common Interview Questions and Answers

Opening Questions

  1. Tell me about yourself.
  2. Why do you want to work for this company?
  3. What interests you about this particular job?
  4. How did you hear about the position?
  5. What do you know about our organization?

Career History Questions

  1. How has your career so far prepared you for this position?
  2. Why do you want to change roles?
  3. What is your greatest professional achievement?
  4. Tell me about your worst boss.
  5. Describe a conflict you faced at work – and how you dealt with it.
  6. How good are you in a crisis?
  7. When have you demonstrated leadership skills?
  8. Describe a time when you showed initiative.
  9. When have you been innovative at work?

Skills/Attributes Questions

  1. Why should we hire you?
  2. What are your greatest strengths?
  3. What makes you unique?

Role-Specific Questions

  1. What are you hoping for from this role?
  2. What would your first 30/60/90 days here look like?
  3. What could you help us to do differently or better?
  4. What would your salary requirements be?

Self-Analysis Questions

  1. When have you failed?
  2. Tell me about a time you made a mistake.
  3. What do you do if you disagree with someone's decision?
  4. Describe a time when you faced an ethical dilemma.
  5. What do you consider to be your weaknesses?
  6. What sort of criticism do you most often receive?

Work-Style Questions

  1. What sorts of things annoy you at work?
  2. How do you tackle difficulties when they arise?
  3. What type of work environment do you prefer?
  4. What's your management style?
  5. If I asked your boss or co-workers about you, what would they say?
  6. How do you deal with pressure?
  7. How do you prioritize your work?
  8. How do you like to be managed?

Goal-Based Questions

  1. Where do you see yourself in five years?
  2. What's your dream job?
  3. What motivates you?

Personal Questions

  1. What do you like to do outside of work?
  2. How do you achieve a good work-life balance?
  3. Are you willing to relocate?

Quirky Questions

  1. How many tennis balls can you fit into a limousine? / How many pennies would be as high as the Empire State building? / How many lightbulbs are on right now in China?
  2. If you were an animal, which one would you want to be?
  3. If you were a superhero, what would be your superpower?
  4. Which five people would you invite to your dream dinner party?
  5. Sell me this pen.

Closing Questions

  1. Is there anything else you'd like us to know?
  2. What other companies are you interviewing with?
  3. When could you start?
  4. Do you have any questions for us?
